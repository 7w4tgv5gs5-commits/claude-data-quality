Overall assessment: The testdb database is in poor health, with pervasive data quality defects across all three tables. The most critical issues are in the orders table, where 61 rows carry negative unit prices (directly corrupting revenue calculations), 58 rows have zero or negative quantities, 19 rows are missing their timestamp, and 20 rows are permanently orphaned from any customer. The customers table contains 20 NULL emails (10% of records), 8 duplicate email addresses, 7 malformed email strings, and 15 rows with impossible dates (future or pre-1900 birthdates). The products catalogue has NULL SKUs and duplicate SKUs on 5 and 6 rows respectively, plus 4 products with negative prices and 2 with NULL prices. The 86 order-to-product price discrepancies — some exceeding $30,000 — are partly explained by historic pricing but partly indicative of data entry corruption. No foreign key constraints are enforced at the schema level, which allows the orphaned rows and NULL FK values to persist silently. Immediate remediation should prioritise: adding NOT NULL + UNIQUE constraints on customers.email and products.sku; adding CHECK constraints to prevent negative prices, prices and quantities; and declaring FK constraints on orders to prevent further referential drift.
